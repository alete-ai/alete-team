## 2. CHANNEL-SPECIFIC OPERATIONAL BLUEPRINTS

### A. Substack (The Long-Form Anchor)
* **Target Audience**: The Alpha-Curator & Tech Professionals.
* **Cadence**: 1x Weekly (1,000–1,500 words).
* **Content Pillars**: Deconstruct digital distraction engineering, screen telemetry research, calm tech philosophy, and focus habit architecture.

### B. LinkedIn (Professional Curation & Thought Leadership)
* **Target Audience**: Knowledge workers, developers, architects, and founders.
* **Cadence**: 3x Weekly.
* **Formats**: 
  1. *PDF Slide Carousels*: 5–7 clean slides breaking down a focus framework using Aris's visual design guidelines.
  2. *Build in Public Posts*: Engineering and product teardowns documenting how Alete silences feed noise.

### C. TikTok & Instagram Reels (Pattern-Interrupt Micro-Videos)
* **Target Audience**: The Optimizer & Digital Ascetic.
* **Cadence**: 5x Weekly (15–30 second duration).
* **The 3-Part Micro-Video Formula**:
  1. **Hook (0–3s)**: High visual contrast (e.g., side-by-side split screen showing a hyper-chaotic social feed vs. Alete's silent, clean interface).
  2. **Retain (3–15s)**: Screen capture demonstration showing real-time element blocking or one-click focus activation.
  3. **Reward (15–30s)**: Actionable tip on digital focus reclamation with a clean, low-pressure CTA.

### D. YouTube Shorts & Organic Search Tutorials
* **Target Audience**: Intent-driven users searching for immediate solutions.
* **Cadence**: 3x Weekly Shorts + 1x Monthly Evergreen Utility Guide.
* **Focus**: Solves specific user friction points (e.g., "How to block Instagram Reels feed on Safari Mac", "How to stop doomscrolling Reddit on iOS").
